Definitions for Compression Calculator Parameters

What do all of those terms mean? Here we go.

  • Number of cylinders

    This one is easy. Just enter the number of cylinders your engine has.

  • Bore

    This is the diameter of the cylinders which are the big holes in the engine block.

  • Stroke

    This is the distance the piston will travel in the cylinder.

  • Rod Length

    The length of the connecting rod when measured from the center of the big end to the center of the small end.

  • Deck Height

    The distance from the crank center line to the top of the cylinder.

  • Chamber Volume

    This is the volume of the combustion chambers in the cylinder head.

  • Piston Volume

    This is the volume of the valve reliefs and dish of the piston. For domed pistons use a negative number here. For dished pistons use a positive number.

  • Piston Compression Height

    This is the distance from the centerline of the wrist pin to the top of the piston. Do not measure from the dome or dish.

  • Gasket Thickness

    The compressed thickness of the head gasket.

  • Gasket Bore

    The diameter of the big holes in the cylinder head gasket.

  • Boost

    The amount of boost you will be running measured in PSI.

  • Altitude

    The distance above sea level that your engine will be operating in.
